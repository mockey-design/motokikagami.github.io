XR/HCI · 3D User Interfaces

Motoki Kagami

Nara Institute of Science and Technology (NAIST)
PhD Student

I’m a 1st-year Ph.D. student at NAIST’s Cybernetics & Reality Engineering Laboratory (CARE Lab), advised by Prof. Kiyoshi Kiyokawa, and a visiting scholar in Prof. Francisco R. Ortega’s NUI Lab at Colorado State University. I'm interested in evaluating 3D user interfaces and understanding what makes them effective in real use. Recently, I've been focusing on context-adaptive 3D interaction in XR—interfaces that adjust to user state and the surrounding environment to remain reliable, efficient, and comfortable across changing situations.
